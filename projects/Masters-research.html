Project Overview

As part of my master’s research at Carnegie Mellon University, I am working under Professor Rebecca Taylor to study the mechanics and locomotion of micro-robotic structures. This research explores new methodologies for simulating and optimizing fluid-structure interactions (FSI) of micro-scale flexible structures, with applications in bio-inspired robotic systems.

My research questions and project goals

  • To develop advanced simulation techniques for fluid-structure interactions in micro-robotic swimmers.
  • To optimize drag and propulsion efficiencies to improve swimmer performance in various fluid environments.
  • To model the electromagnetic forces that drive swimmer movement and analyze the impacts on swimmer dynamics.

Results and Findings

My preliminary findings indicate that optimization of micro-swimmer shapes can significantly reduce drag, enhancing speed and overall locomotion efficiency. The study has shown that specific design alterations lead to improved fluid dynamics, paving the way for effective micro-robotic applications in medical and environmental monitoring.
